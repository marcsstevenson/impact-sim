Persona: Dr Emily Ngata

Role: Clinical Director, Alpine Community Medical Centre

Location: Small alpine township, South Island, New Zealand

Population Served: Approximately 5,800 permanent residents plus seasonal workers and tourists.

Background

Dr Emily Ngata has been the Clinical Director for six years.

She is:

An experienced rural GP.
A respected leader in the community.
Responsible for clinical governance and emergency planning.
One of only two doctors in town.
Married with two teenage children at the local secondary school.
Her partner manages a nearby sheep and beef farm.
She is trained to make difficult medical decisions—but not decisions affecting hundreds of people simultaneously.

Medical Centre

Staff on duty:

2 General Practitioners
2 Registered Nurses
2 Healthcare Assistants
1 Receptionist
1 Practice Manager
1 Community Pharmacist (located next door)
Resources:

3 treatment rooms
1 resuscitation room
Small generator
Limited oxygen
Small medication store
Limited blood products
One satellite phone
Four-wheel-drive vehicle
Core Leadership Challenge

"How do I provide the greatest benefit to the greatest number of people while every patient, every family and every member of my staff believes their situation is the highest priority?"

Earthquake Scenario

At 10:34 a.m., a magnitude 7.9 Alpine Fault earthquake strikes.

Immediate impacts:

Severe structural damage across town.
Roads closed in multiple directions.
Power failure.
Water supply disrupted.
Telecommunications intermittent.
Ambulances unable to reach the township.
Hospital over 90 minutes away.
Helicopters unavailable due to weather.
Heavy snow forecast overnight.
Within 45 minutes more than 60 casualties begin arriving.

Immediate Situation

Current patients include:

Multiple fractures
Crush injuries
Head injuries
Major bleeding
Heart attack
Labouring mother (36 weeks pregnant)
Child with severe asthma
Elderly patients requiring oxygen
Dialysis patients unable to travel
Residents requiring insulin refrigeration
The waiting room is overflowing.

The carpark becomes an unofficial triage area.

Core Leadership Responsibilities

Dr Ngata must simultaneously:

Lead clinical decision-making.
Direct staff.
Prioritise patients.
Manage scarce resources.
Liaise with Civil Defence.
Communicate with families.
Protect exhausted staff.
Prepare for a multi-day response.

65 patients present.

Dilemma

Remain at the medical centre?

Or leave briefly to locate family?

She may never forgive herself.

Decision

Hardest Lesson

Modern disaster medicine is rarely about providing the best care to every individual.

It is about providing the greatest achievable benefit across an entire affected community when demand vastly exceeds available resources.

Every decision creates moral injury.

Every decision leaves someone waiting.

Every decision is made with incomplete information.

The challenge is not finding the perfect answer—it is making transparent, ethical and defensible decisions while preserving trust, compassion and clinical judgement under extreme pressure.

Learning Objective: Players should appreciate the extraordinary burden carried by rural clinical leaders during major disasters, where medicine, ethics, leadership and community expectations collide. They should leave understanding that many of the hardest decisions are not medical—they are human.

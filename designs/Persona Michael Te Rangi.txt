Persona: Michael Te Rangi

Role: Managing Director, Alpine Utilities Ltd

Utility: Regional electricity distribution network

Location: South Island, New Zealand

Customers Served: Approximately 42,000 homes and businesses across alpine towns, farming districts and remote communities.

Core Leadership Challenge

"Every hour without electricity increases the risk to lives, businesses and critical infrastructure. How do I restore the network safely when every decision leaves someone without power?"

Earthquake Scenario

At 10:34 a.m., a magnitude 7.9 Alpine Fault earthquake strikes.

Within minutes:

Multiple transmission faults.
Five substations offline.
Hundreds of protection trips.
Unknown damage to mountain transmission towers.
Fibre communications partially lost.
SCADA visibility degraded.
Fuel deliveries interrupted.
Roads blocked by landslides.
Helicopters grounded due to weather.
Nearly 38,000 customers lose power.

Hardest Lesson

The Managing Director never has enough:

Information.
Crews.
Fuel.
Time.
Money.
Equipment.
Every restoration decision creates winners and losers.

Restoring electricity is not an engineering problem—it is a leadership problem involving ethics, politics, economics, safety and public trust.

The objective is not simply to restore power as quickly as possible.

It is to restore the greatest level of community function while ensuring no worker is unnecessarily placed in harm's way.

Learning Objective: Players should leave understanding that utility leaders make some of the most consequential yet least visible decisions during disasters. Their choices determine whether hospitals function, water remains safe, communications continue, businesses survive and communities recover—all while balancing safety, public expectations and competing critical needs.
